# Effects of FoV Strategy and Locomotion Interface in Active-Navigation Mixed Reality with Dynamic Peripheral Real-World View
Prior Virtual Reality (VR) research has examined Field-of-View (FoV) modulation and locomotion, and passthrough Mixed Reality (MR) has highlighted the potential of real-world cue exposure. However, less is known about how these factors affect active navigation when motion-contingent peripheral real-world visibility is present. We conducted a within-subject user study crossing FoV strategy and locomotion interface in an MR setting where dynamic Peripheral Real-World View (PRV) was held constant across conditions.
Our results show dynamic FoV was associated with higher noticeability of peripheral visual changes, whereas locomotion interface was associated with differences in object-recognition hit rate. No reliable differences in cybersickness were observed across conditions. These findings suggest that patterns reported in passive or static-PRV paradigms should not be assumed to generalize directly to active-navigation contexts with motion-contingent PRV, and they motivate future studies that manipulate PRV as an independent factor.
